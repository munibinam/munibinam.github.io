## Research Focus
I am a PhD candidate in **Agricultural Economics** at the University of Kentucky. My work integrates applied econometrics, high‑resolution climate data, and spatial analysis to quantify how extreme weather reshapes U.S. agriculture and drinking‑water systems. Ultimately, I aim to deliver reproducible, policy‑ready tools for a hotter and wetter world.

### Current Projects
| Theme | Key Question | Core Methods |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| **Climate Metrics & Bias** | Do spatial interpolation techniques hide the true cost of extreme heat? | Station vs. grid comparisons, fixed‑effects yield models |
| **Water Quality Under Stress** | How do sub‑daily weather shocks trigger drinking‑water violations? | Rare‑events logit, high‑frequency climate records |
| **Water Demand & Urban Form** | What role does housing lot size play in drought‑era consumption? | Panel regressions, utility micro‑data |
